Company Services and Locations

Diyala State Company provides its services through various locations in Diyala Governorate. Learn about the available services and contact information for each location.

Main Headquarters

The company's headquarters is located at the main entrance to Diyala Governorate center, near Al-Quds intersection leading to all districts of the governorate and on the international road connecting Diyala Governorate with the capital, Baghdad.

  • Phone: +964 781 123 4567
  • Email: info@diala.industry.gov.iq
  • Working Hours: Sunday - Thursday, 8:00 AM - 3:00 PM

Vocational Training Center

The vocational training center at Diyala Company provides specialized training services in electrical and electronic industries, and receives trainees from company employees and external entities.

Services Provided:

  1. Training courses in electrical industries
  2. Transformer and meter maintenance courses
  3. Occupational safety courses
  4. Administrative and technical skills development courses

Research and Development Unit

The Research and Development Unit works on developing existing products and creating new products that meet the needs of the local market, with a focus on sustainability and energy efficiency.

Areas of Work:

  • Development and improvement of existing products
  • Testing materials and products
  • Collaboration with universities and research centers
  • Feasibility studies for new products

Challenges Facing the Company

Major obstacles affecting the company's performance and operations

Marketing Difficulties

Difficulty in marketing products due to intense competition in the local market caused by random importing and failure to implement product protection laws despite their approval

Raw Materials Shortage

Difficulty in providing certain imported raw materials that are critical for production

Loss of Expertise

Lack of knowledge transfer between staff due to limited recruitment opportunities for young talent, with many workers, technicians, and engineers retiring, leading to continuous decline in expertise and skills

Debt Collection Issues

Difficulty in collecting debts from companies affiliated with the Ministry of Electricity, which is the main customer of the company, negatively affecting the company's cash flow needed to provide production requirements
